General facts

  • Races: Mountain people (dwarves or something), humins (humans), gobos (goblins).
  • Beasts: small, medium, large, very large. Some tameable. Many edible.
  • Availability of magic: [None, Rare, Common] - this affects how many races and creatures will have magical abilities.
  • Minerals: ...
  • Liquids: ...
  • Tool and weapon craft: stone, wood, metal etc
  • Flow of time: 1 game iteration equals to 2 hours. 12 iterations - 1 day, ~360 iterations - a month, ~4300 iterations - a year.

Technology

  • Swords, maces, polearms, daggers
  • Simple mining and crafting tools (stone, wood, metal+)
  • Archery, limited gun models
  • Simple humin-sized mechanisms
  • Large mechanisms (drawbridges etc) require some power, river power maybe.
  • Brick-laying, digging, carving - that sort of building is available.
  • Agriculture: growing grains, normal and root vegetables, vines, shrooms.

Professions and skills

Every character can take any skill. Affinities give bonus to few certain random skill, but that just saves time while training. A skill that is not used for 6-12+ months is losing points (rusts). Higher complexity crafts take increasingly more time and materials with low skill.

Military skills

  • Archer
  • Gunner
  • One-handed weapons
  • Two-handed weapons
  • Trapper (both enemy and animals)

Peaceful skills

  • Miner
  • Smith
  • Carpenter/woodworker
  • Cook
  • Stonemason
  • Medic
  • Militia/enforcer
  • Farmer (both animals and plants). Things to consider: some people, me included, would like to minimize farming. And some other people enjoy it.

Management

Player's control over the world is limited. Player can give commands, WHAT he wants changed in the world. Characters select orders that they prefer and try to build a plan, how to do the change.

Example of desired changes: mining (want this block be not solid), want a ramp, want to produce N of item M. Example of characters' own desires: Hungry (want to increase satiety), sleepy (want to increase rested), bored (want to increase entertained).

  • Define zones for storage (select what goes where), for sleep, for partying and eating, for farming.
